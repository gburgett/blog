# 2026-03-20 Scrum is Broken

I thought I knew what Agile looked like. 2-week sprints, story points, daily standups. I ran that cadence at my last job and it worked.

Then Opus 4.7 happened.

Now, once we've groomed the cards and defined the sprint, everything is done within a day. Doesn't matter how many cards we throw into a sprint, we just fire up more parallel agents.

The points don't matter anymore.

So here's the question I've been wrestling with: how do you manage a team when the cost of writing code trends to zero? What do your software engineers do between prompts?

We went back to first principles.

**Scrum was never the goal**

Scrum is one attempt to implement the Agile Manifesto, built on the assumption that implementation takes the bulk of the time. That assumption is dead. When a process stops working for your team, the Manifesto is clear: value individuals and interactions over processes and tools.

So we threw the ceremonies out and went back to Lean Software Development principles to rebuild.

**Where we landed**

We settled on three principles to prioritize:

1. Empower the team to own the complete life cycle of a system or subsystem
2. Amplify learning by shortening cycle times
3. Eliminate waste by reducing middlemen in communication

The biggest shift: we now view each team member as a product owner over a specific system or subsystem, managing a team of agents to do the actual implementation.

The scrum ceremonies were always waste. They were just necessary waste. When implementation gets cheap enough, you get to ask which of your "necessary" waste is still necessary.
